About

I'm a graduate student at the University of Trieste completing my Master's in Data Science, with a background in cognitive psychology and psycholinguistics. Currently writing my thesis on NLP-based analysis of psychological discourse with graduation due in March 2026

Language fascinates me, and I always wonder if we (so called) humans can ever manage to fully fathom (or model) the linguistic structures in our brains or the cognitive processes that lie behind it (it is fascinating already that we are using language to do that). In the meantime I am trying to study the methods and the theory whether in computational linguistics, psycholinguistics, mere semantics/logic, or philosophy of language, in an attempt to have better tools for such a mission, naturally, I am curious about LLMs and their interpretability (XAI), especially regarding what they can—and cannot—reveal about how we think. I'm particularly interested in fair AI, the representation of low-resource languages, and the methodological insights we can gain from studying these systems' limitations and capabilities. In my free time I enjoy reading novels and rock climbing.
